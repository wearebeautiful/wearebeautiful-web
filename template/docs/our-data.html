Our data explained

We've taken great care in keeping track of the right data, keeping the needed data private to protect the identity of our models and making this data useful.

Model codes

All of our models are carefully coded with the URL of this site and a model code:

453703-VLNN-1

453703

identifies the human model. You can assume that the models 453703-VLNN and 453703-BSNN come from the same human.

VLNN

identifies the body part, pose, arrangement and excited status of the model. For this example, we have a vulva model, lying down, naturally arranged, not excited. While body part and pose are simple to understand, arrangement and excited need some explanation.

Arrangement concerns if the model has been arranged in any way. Vulva models are a great example; as some vulvas emerge from underwear the may be folded or their shape influenced by being constrained in underwear. Not arranging a vulva in any way, is considered natural. If a vulva is arranged, perhaps to be symmetrical, it is considered arranged. For some models, like penises, a model may also have the foreskin retracted, in which case the arrangement is retracted. Compare this naturally arranged vulva with the same vulva that was arragnged to be spread apart. Or compare this not-retracted penis to this retracted penis.

Excited takes into account if the model is sexually excited at the time the pictures were taken. This is less important for vulva models, but clearly important for penis models.

The three data layers

We've collected three data layers:

  1. This inner layer is the identity, age verification and signature that indicates their consent to participate in this project. This data only exists on paper under lock and key and is not online, at all.
  2. The middle layer of data contains age, country of origin and self declared ethniticty of each model. This data is only shown to the public in aggregate to show the diversity of our models. This data is not public data, but we may consider releasing it to researchers at qualified research institutions.
  3. The outer layer of data contains all the public data you find on this site. Hopefully this data is not at all useful for identifying the models.
